> **Purpose of this file.** This is a reference document for any AI system (caption generation, content scheduling, copywriting) working on A.won content. Read it in full before generating any text. When in doubt, follow the rules in *Brand Voice* and *Hard Rules* over any other instinct.

## 1. What A.won is — in one paragraph

A.won is a **concept-only architecture studio** based in **New Delhi**, founded by **Abhishek RK Khanna** (Founder & Principal Architect). The studio designs residences, retreats, farmhouses, villas, hotels, and resorts for discerning clients across Delhi and India, and is increasingly engaged across the GCC (UAE, Saudi Arabia) and with NRI clients. A.won delivers the *design* — the idea, plans, sections, three-dimensional form, and cinematic visualisation — and partners with executive architects of record for construction documentation. It does not produce construction drawings. The work is distinguished by **restraint, material honesty, and a precise attention to how natural light moves through a space across the day.**

## 5. Design philosophy (the substance behind the captions)

1. **Restraint over decoration.** Every move is earned. The discipline of removal is where premium architecture begins.
2. **Story before form.** Before designing a wall, design a moment — the threshold, the reveal, the room you want to live in.
3. **Light as material.** How sunlight enters a room at four o'clock is a design decision, not an accident. Every project is modelled across the day before openings are committed.

Public-facing philosophy words also used on the website: **Simplicity, Harmony, Longevity.**

## 7. Brand voice — non-negotiable

- **Tone:** Restrained, premium, specific, confident. Never promotional. Never salesy.

## 8. Pinterest strategy — how content must behave

**Pinterest is a visual search engine, not a social feed.** Discovery comes from keyword-rich, natural-language titles and descriptions — NOT from hashtags. Hashtags are a near-dead discovery lever on Pinterest; keep them minimal and clean. The growth levers, in order of impact:

1. **Keyword-led titles** matching real HNI search intent ("Travertine living room in a modern Indian villa", "Sculptural stone staircase", "Courtyard farmhouse facade in Delhi").
2. **Keyword-rich descriptions** in natural prose — weave in the terms clients search: villa, residence, farmhouse, interior, courtyard, facade, travertine, stone, Delhi, India (only where genuinely relevant).
3. **Fresh pins consistently** — cadence beats bursts.
4. **Board names that are themselves keywords.**

**Current state:** ~8.3k followers. Goal: organic growth via search reach, not engagement-bait.

### Caption field contract (for the generator)
- **title** — ≤100 chars. Keyword-led, open with strongest searchable terms. No hashtags, no emoji.
- **description** — ≤500 chars TOTAL (prose + hashtags). Restrained premium prose, keyword-rich, may close with a quiet signature like *"Concept by A.won, New Delhi."* Then 5–8 clean hashtags. Trim prose first so hashtags are never cut off.
- **alt_text** — ≤500 chars. Plain factual description of what's visible only. No hashtags, no brand language, no studio name.

### Hashtag set (secondary lever — premium only)
`#conceptarchitecture` `#indianarchitecture` `#luxuryvilla` `#modernindianhome` `#villadesign` `#interiordesign` `#travertine` `#stoneinteriors` `#delhiarchitecture` `#architecturedesign`

**Never use:** `#archilovers` `#archi` `#instagood` `#follow4follow` or any engagement-farming tag (these signal mid-tier and hurt positioning).

## 9. Hard rules for any AI generating A.won content

1. **Never invent facts.** Do not fabricate project names, locations, areas, prices, client names, awards, publications, dates, or studio statistics. Describe only what is visible in the image.
2. **Do not put statistics or credentials into captions** (e.g. "X projects", "X years experience"). They read promotional and undercut the premium tone. Context shapes voice; it does not become copy.
3. **No discount or hype language. Ever.** See the banned-words list in Section 7.
4. **No off-brand output.** If it doesn't look or sound like A.won, it doesn't publish.
5. **No client work referenced without permission.** NDA-protected projects stay private.
6. **British/Indian English spelling** throughout.
7. **Honour character limits exactly** — especially the 500-char total on `description` including hashtags.
